# 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing hotel booking behavior, cancellation risk patterns, occupancy demand, and revenue drivers using Power BI.

The objective was to transform raw hotel reservation data into an interactive business intelligence dashboard capable of helping hotel management teams improve:

- Revenue forecasting
- Occupancy planning
- Booking reliability analysis
- Pricing strategy optimization
- Customer demand understanding

The dashboard was designed from a hospitality revenue management perspective to support data-driven operational and financial decision-making.

---

# 🎯 Business Problem

Hotels rely heavily on reservation forecasting to optimize occupancy, staffing, and pricing strategies. However, high cancellation rates create operational uncertainty and revenue instability.

Some of the major business challenges include:

- High booking cancellations affecting occupancy forecasting
- Limited visibility into reliable booking channels
- Difficulty identifying high-revenue room categories
- Lack of understanding of customer stay behavior

This project aims to address these challenges by analyzing booking reliability, revenue performance, and customer demand patterns through interactive Power BI reporting.

# 📈 Key Business Insights

## Booking Cancellation Risk

The analysis reveals that:
- 32.76% of bookings are cancelled
- Nearly one out of every three reservations does not result in an actual stay
- High cancellation rates create uncertainty in occupancy forecasting and revenue planning

---

## Booking Channel Performance

### Online Booking Channels
Online platforms generate the largest booking volume but also contribute the highest number of cancellations.

- Non-cancelled bookings: 14.7K
- Cancelled bookings: 8.5K

This indicates lower booking commitment levels and higher flexibility in online reservations.

### Offline Booking Channels

Offline channels demonstrate relatively more stable booking behavior.

- Non-cancelled bookings: 7.4K
- Cancelled bookings: 3.2K

### Corporate Bookings

Corporate reservations show the highest reliability.

- Non-cancelled bookings: 1.8K
- Cancelled bookings: 0.2K

This suggests stronger booking commitment driven by business travel requirements.

## Meal Plan Preferences

Meal Plan 1 dominates customer demand with 28K bookings.

Other selections include:

## Weekday vs Weekend Demand

Demand analysis reveals that weekday stays dominate hotel bookings.

- Weekday Demand: 73.21%
- Weekend Demand: 26.79%

This suggests strong influence from:
- Business travel
- Corporate stays
- Extended weekday reservations
